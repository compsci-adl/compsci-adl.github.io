Game Comp

Programming Competition

Overview

Game Comp is a programming competition where indidividuals or teams design and develop their own game, for glory and prizes.

The competition spans over the mid-semester break (3 weeks), being a good opportunity to take a break, have some fun making a game and potentially create something to pad your resume while your at it.

There is a central theme that every game has to use, but it's open and up to each teams interpretation. No design documents or pretty code required, this isn't your homework. All we care about is your final product in all its glory and bugginess.

Theme

Your submission should aim to incorporate our theme for the year. You do not have to strictly hold to the theme, rather, we encourage creative interpretations on it.

The theme for this year is:

Timeline

Semester 3 Winners annouced + possible event to showcase/decide on community choice
Entries are allowed at any point during the competition. Want to throw something together the day before? Go ahead!

Top Tips
  • Don't think it has to be perfect, like at all. Doesn't have sound? Cool. You're using literal hitboxes as enemies? Excellent
  • Start small. Start with your core features, then slowly add more and more
  • Games are great for resumes as they show a passion for tech, but they can be even better if you incorporate some technical complexity
  • Have some fun! It's only three weeks, just try and submit something, even if it's quarter-baked.
Judging Each entry will be judged by a small panel. Ideally, we would like to play each of your games, but if your game doesn't compile easily or you don't think 5-10mins would really showcase your game you will be required to submit a video to highlight your game instead. Source code will need to be provided for all entries, but will not be scrutinized for code style.

Criteria The submissions will be based around the following criteria:
  • Fun factor and game concept
  • Aesthetics/feel
  • Technical complexity
  • Theme involvement
Prizes Major Prizes
There will be a minimum prize pool of $100, which may increase (but not decrease).
  • Golden Egg. The best game out of all the teams.
  • Golden Egg: Duckling. The best game from a team of first-years.
  • People's Choice. Publicly voted best game out of all games.
Minor Prizes may be added

Rules

Language and Libraries
  • Can use any free programming language (eg: C++, Processing, Javascript).
  • Can use any library or framework (must be included in source).
  • Can NOT use pre-existing engine (eg: Unity, GameMaker, Unreal Engine)
  • Strive to be able to run on University systems, both performance wise and technical wise.
  • Not sure? just ask
Teams and Participants
  • Teams can be of up to 3 people.
  • Participants do NOT have to be a computer science students.
  • Participants must be current students at time of competition start.
  • Participants do not have to be from the same year level.
  • A team can only qualify as a "First-year team", if all of the participants are in their first year of computer science or equivalent (e.g Soft eng.).
  • The majority of the team must be from the University of Adelaide. Any other participants still must be students.
  • To be in the running for prizes, all members must be members of the CS club (can join after submissions)
Submission
  • Source code must be available for review and judging.
  • An accompanying video, demonstrating your game and all its major features is optional. Upload the video to some streaming service (eg: YouTube.com, Vimeo.com), and then provide the link.
  • A text README is also required, that includes the following:
    • Instructions on how to build and run the game.
    • Dependencies required to build the game.
    • Game controls and instructions.
    • Any features that you'd like to point out.
    • Team name and team members.
  • Submission of code is done by any of the following:
    • Web link submission (eg: github.com)
    • Email to competition@csclub.org.au
    • Physical hand-in to committee member (Daniel)
Your Work, Assets and Licensing

We want to see YOUR work! You may have a great idea to recreate Asteroids, or have your own Overwatch, but we want to encourage you away from that. We want to see the coolest, most innovative design you and your team can create. Even if your game isn't finished in the end, it's the overall concept that has the most impact.

As there are real prizes you can win, and we want you to showcase your work, you will need to take care with the assets that you use. Any images, music, libraries or similar that you may use, must be free for use, for commercial or non-commercial use. If in doubt, look at the licensing or email the link/asset with its license to competition@csclub.org.au. You can of course make your own assets if you want!

Any submission you make, still belongs to you. We are NOT going to steal it and claim it. However, if we really like your submission, we may ask you if we can use your submission or video in future showcases.
