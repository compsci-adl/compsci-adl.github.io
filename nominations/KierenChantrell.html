2018 Committee Nomination

Kieren Chantrell

Second year student and tutor studying B. Computer Science (Advanced)

Position(s)

  • Vice President
  • Secretary

What will you bring to the CS Club?

I have experience with event planning, scheduling and marketing, all essential skills to increase the visibility of the CS club and be an effective Vice President. I am also a good communicator, and am comfortable speaking to people, to a room and in public.

Why do you want to be on the committee?

Community is an integral part of the university experience; I wish to be as involved with the CS community as much I can, as well as make the club as accessible and enticing for other students in CS. My enrolment in computer science is a natural indicator of my enthusiasm for the field as a whole, and I would like to help create an avenue for like-minded people to commingle within the university and involve themselves in the wider field outside university. Leadership, as with my desire to be Vice President, is a fulfilling experience which allows you to be part of something greater than yourself, while also being challenging, and an excellent platform to learn new skills and gain new experiences.

What previous experience do you have that will help you serve the club?

I currently have a tutoring role which requires a good understanding of and ability to present CS concepts, as well as being able to engage one-on-one with students. I was awarded a summer scholarship for a project to interface object recognition with a NAO humanoid robot, which ultimately required knowledge of advanced programming concepts, of which I had to independently learn and execute. This demonstrates my diligence, self-sufficiency, and willingness to learn new concepts, all essential traits for a good leader. I placed 3rd alongside my sister in an international competition, the HeroX NASA Sky For All challenge, to redesign the airspace for 2035 and beyond, despite competing against professionals in the aerospace industry. This shows my ability to think of creative, novel and - importantly - viable solutions to problems, which will serve well the CS club. My engagement with the wider CS community and confidence in public is clear through my volunteering at Open Day and participation in the upcoming Ingenuity event. I have also been elected marketing manager for UniSA IPITS entrepreneurship club, a position demanding leadership, initiative and marketing skills, all of which will excellently serve a role as Vice President of the CS club.
